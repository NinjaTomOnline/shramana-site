A puzzle pilgrimage where only one monk reaches the gate.

Shramana Project is an iPad-only SpriteKit and SwiftUI puzzle game inspired by classic sacrifice-routing design. Monks walk, wander, pray, levitate, chant, teleport, and give themselves up so a single survivor can pass through the Nirvana Gate.

Four chapters, one impossible exit

Every puzzle is a small teaching with teeth.

The campaign moves from forest foothills through temple courtyards, bamboo mountains, mirror caves, rooftops, and a cosmic dreamscape. Each level opens with a koan, then turns that idea into a tactical routing problem.

Chapter I

The Barrier of Mu

Beginnings, meditation blockers, prayer bridges, levitation, and first sacrifices.

Chapter II

Compassionate Action

Kung fu barriers, fox-switch karma, moving platforms, and lantern routes.

Chapter III

Illusion of Self

Invisible platforms, mirrors, pressure plates, and astral switch work.

Chapter IV

The Gateless Gate

Portals, paradox routes, all-systems convergence, and the quiet epilogue.

Ability tokens

Small verbs, large consequences.

Meditate

Become a living blocker and redirect the crowd.

Pray

Call temporary bridges and hidden platforms into being.

Levitate

Survive deadly drops or cross a dangerous gap.

Kung Fu

Break barriers when compassion needs a sharp edge.

Astral

Leave the body briefly to hit unreachable switches.

Teleport

Thread the final chapter through portal sequences.

Stop Chanting

Freeze timing hazards and moving platforms at the right breath.

Sacrifice

Spend a monk so another can make the final step.

PixelLab production art

Earthy, side-view, meditative, but still crunchy at puzzle scale.

The site uses the same generated monk frames, UI icons, parallax backdrops, portals, lanterns, and gate objects that are integrated into the game project. The visual promise and the runtime assets now speak the same language.

Release status

The app target exists. The public store page is next.
